<section style='border: 2px solid rgb(123, 12, 0);padding:15px;line-height: 1.8;font-size: 15px;font-family: PingFangSC-Light, "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica, "Microsoft YaHei", 微软雅黑, Arial, SimHei;'>
<h3 style="line-height:1.8;color:rgb(123, 12, 0);text-align:center">张爱玲的英语水准怎么样？</h3>
<p style="line-height:1.8;color:rgb(123, 12, 0);text-align:center">创作有时因无材料或思想枯竭而无从产生，<br> 
为练习写作起见， <br>
翻译是很有助于文字的技术的，<br>
假如你的英文不过于糟， <br>
不妨自己随便试试。 <br>
<br>
by朱生豪<br>
（《莎士比亚戏剧全集》译者）</p>	
<p>张爱玲，说是中国文学史上的传奇不过分，不过国文考试曾不及格，终其一生无文凭。事实是不仅中文造诣极高， 英文也是一流。</p>
<p>获得《纽约时报》好评的小说《秧歌》(The Rice Sprout Song, Yang Ge)也是先英文，后才译为中文。</p>
<p>最初也是靠写英文谋生，也有英文杂志的稿费高于中文杂志的原因。 这些投稿文章多为谈中国文化（cultural critiques），影评(film reviews)等。</p>
<p>比如给英文报纸《上海泰晤士报》（Shanghai Times）投影评， 剧评。比如《鸦片战争》（The Opium War）。以下截取了部分。</p>
<div style="color:#888">
The film is already crowded with dramatic material. The excuse for adding a romance “which might have been” as a subplot is probably to provide a role for the Manchoukuo star Lee Hsiang-lan. With her songs, Miss Lee proves to be the most eloquent spokesman of the piece; for the dialogue, though polished and concise, is not free from the doubtful mixing of modern phraseology with the classical language—the usual trouble with costume plays.
<p style="text-align:right">By Eileen Chang(张爱玲)</p>	
<p>故事本身已不乏戏剧性的材料。影片中“想当然”的爱情故事大概是为安排满映的李香兰的演出而加上去的。李小姐的歌艺使她成为片中所演角色最适当的人选。片中对话虽然精确洗练，但仍文白参半，是古装剧常有的毛病。</p>
</div>
后来德国人办的英文杂志《二十世纪》（《The XXth Century》）也向她约稿。 
<p>写的是“中国的生活与时装”(China’s life and fashions)，讲的是从清代到民国初期女子的衣装转变， 后来汉化为《更衣记》(A Chronicle of Changing Clothes)。</p>
<p>这些文章中大多采用一种轻俏的调侃语气，向外国人阐释中国文化，介绍中国人的生活。 这些文章备受好评，被主编赞为“最有前途的青年天才”。</p>
<div style="color:#888">	
... that when she returned to Shanghai in 1942 and tried to enroll in St. John's University she failed to get in because her Chinese grades were too low. But her English was so good that her early paid writings were film criticism pieces she wrote in 1942 for the only English language daily in Shanghai at the time, Shanghai Times. She also wrote essays for magazines for the English readership in China on aspects of Chinese life. 
<p>Essays such as “Chinese Life and Fashions”(1943), published in The XXth Century, demonstrated her increasing awareness of how the mundane in Chinese culture could interest foreigners.</p>
<p style="text-align:right">出自Eileen Chang:<br> Romancing Languages,<br> Cultures and Genres</p>		
</div>
<p>后来的写作生涯里，张爱玲的文章有不少中英文都是互相改写。 这种能力， Nora好生佩服和羡慕。 总结一下，就是</p>
<p><h3 style="line-height:1.8;color:rgb(123, 12, 0);text-align:center">把优秀的英文翻译成中文， 再翻译回去。</h3></p>
<p>若是再加上,<span style="font-weight:900;line-height:1.8;color:rgb(123, 12, 0);text-align:center">拿自己的英文和原文做对比，定能迅速提升英文能力，比如写作能力。</span></p>
<p>“靠写作改变命运” 的美国国父富兰克林就是这么做的。 他模仿的是英国作家Joseph Addison的文字。</p>
<p>嗯，这个方法一定有奇效，但是真正练习的有多少？魔鬼营社群每天都会有日签，精选经典好句，该如何用能帮助提高英文？不如今天试一下富兰克林的方法。</p>
